TARZAN!

Taong Gubat

Tarzan stands as the ultimate bridge between the primeval and the aristocratic, a character defined by the "noble savage" archetype. Born into British nobility as John Clayton but forged by the harsh realities of the African jungle, he embodies a unique fusion of raw physical instinct and high-level intellect. While he possesses the predatory grace and strength of the Great Apes who raised him, he remains an outlier in both worldsâ€”too human for the wild and too untamed for the "civilized" drawing rooms of London. This duality makes him a deeply introspective figure who often views human society through a lens of skepticism, preferring the honesty of nature over the perceived hypocrisy of modern man. Ultimately, Tarzan is a survivor whose true strength lies not just in his muscles, but in his unyielding moral code and his perpetual search for a place to truly belong.

Origins: In 1888, John and Alice Clayton are marooned on the West African coast after a mutiny. Following their deaths, their infant son is adopted by Kala, a female of a fictional ape species known as the Mangani.

Youth: Named Tarzan ("White-Skin"), the boy develops extraordinary physical strength and agility. He discovers his parents' abandoned cabin and, using their books, teaches himself to read and write English, though he cannot yet speak it.

The Dilemma: Tarzan eventually travels to the West to find Jane. While he discovers he is the rightful heir to the Greystoke estate, he initially conceals his identity to ensure Jane's financial security when he finds she is engaged to his cousin.
